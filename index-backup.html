🚀 Quick Start Essentials

Model: TurboChef High H Batch 2 (HHB-2)
Capacity: Full sheet pan (18" × 13") or 16" pizza
Power: 208V or 240V (auto-detects)

First Time Setup

  1. Press power button (left side)
  2. Wait 8-10 minutes for "Ready to Cook"
  3. Enable Edit Menu (hold top soft key 5 seconds)

Basic Operation Flow

Power On → Warm Up → Select Group → Select Item → Place Food → Auto Cook → Remove

⚡ Understanding the Technology

What "High h" Means

h = heat transfer coefficient

Transfers heat 3-4x more efficiently than regular convection ovens

How It Cooks

  • 60 mph air jets from top & bottom
  • Rack oscillates 2.5" side-to-side
  • 500°F maximum temperature
  • Catalytic converter for ventless operation

Temperature Zones

Temperature Best For Examples

475°F Standard proteins Chicken, turkey, beef

Air Speed Settings

📝 Step-by-Step Programming

IMPORTANT: Oven must be OFF or cooling to program recipes!

Enable Edit Mode First

  1. Hold top-left or top-right soft key for 5 seconds
  2. Select "Edit Menu" → Turn ON
  3. Press BACK to exit

Programming Sequence

IMPORTANT FIRST STEPS:
• Oven MUST be OFF or cooling down
• Enable Edit Menu first (hold top corner soft key 5 seconds)
• Press "Edit Menu" soft key to turn ON
• Press BACK/STOP to exit options menu
  1. Enter Programming: Hold UP arrow 1 second
  2. Set Temperature: Use UP/DOWN, press ENTER
  3. Select Group: Press soft key under group
  4. Select Slot: Press soft key under item
  5. Enter Details:
    • Name (use number keys like old phone)
    • Time (UP/DOWN in 5-sec increments)
    • Rack (ON/OFF)
    • Events (% time and % air)
  6. Save: Press ENTER

Number Keypad Text Entry

2 = ABC    5 = JKL    8 = TUV
3 = DEF    6 = MNO    9 = WXYZ
4 = GHI    7 = PQRS   0 = 0
          1 = Space

Detailed Button-by-Button Programming Example

Example: Programming Frozen Turkey Leg (Small)

  1. Hold UP arrow 1 second (enter programming)
  2. Press UP until "475" shows
  3. Press ENTER
  4. Press soft key under "1ST GROUP"
  5. Press soft key under "1ST ITEM"
  6. Name entry: T-U-R-K [space] L-E-G [space] S
    • Press 8 once = T
    • Press 8 twice = U
    • Press 7 twice = R
    • Press 5 twice = K
    • Press 1 = space
    • Continue for LEG S...
  7. Press RIGHT arrow to time field
  8. Press UP 216 times for 18:00
  9. Press RIGHT to Rack, press UP for ON
  10. Set Events: 70% time @ 90% air, then 30% @ 100%
  11. Press ENTER to save

🧹 Cleaning & Maintenance

CRITICAL: Daily cleaning is NON-NEGOTIABLE! Prevents 90% of problems.

3-Minute Daily Cleaning

  1. Wait for "Ready to Clean" (below 150°F)
  2. Remove wire rack
  3. Spray cavity with TurboChef cleaner
  4. Let sit 30 seconds
  5. Wipe with damp cloth
  6. Remove & rinse bottom jetplate (lift straight up)
  7. Rinse grease filter (gentle - no scrubbing!)
  8. Reinstall everything
  9. Spray Oven Guard lightly
  10. Close door - Done!

  • CLEAN IMMEDIATELY
  • Check grease filter
  • Clean jetplates

  • Don't overcrowd
  • Clean jetplates
  • Single layer only

🔴 Too Dark/Burnt

  • Reduce time 20%
  • Lower to 450°F

  • Check cook mode

🔄 Understanding Rack Oscillation

What Does RACK ON/OFF Mean?

RACK ON = Moving/Oscillating

  • Rack moves slowly side-to-side
  • 2.5" total movement
  • 8-second cycle time
  • Ensures even exposure to air jets

RACK OFF = Stationary

  • Rack stays completely still
  • Food remains in one position
  • Creates "leopard spots"
  • Rarely used (only for melting)
BOTTOM LINE: Leave RACK ON for 99% of cooking. It's essential for even browning!

🍳 Pan vs Directly on Rack

✅ Directly on Rack (BEST)

  • Turkey legs
  • Whole potatoes
  • Egg rolls
  • Chicken pieces
  • Frozen snacks
  • Most proteins

Benefits: Fastest cooking, best browning, even cooking all around

⚠️ When to Use a Pan

  • Items with excessive dripping
  • Small items that fall through
  • Delicate fish
  • Items in sauce/liquid
  • Raw bacon (grease management)

Note: Add 15-25% more cook time with pan

💨 Smoke When Opening - Is It Normal?

✅ Normal Smoke

  • Light wisp when door opens
  • Fatty proteins (turkey legs)
  • High-fat frozen items
  • Items at 475-500°F
  • First few uses if new

⚠️ NOT Normal

  • Thick billowing smoke
  • Smoke during entire cycle
  • Acrid/burning smell
  • Smoke with lean foods
#1 CAUSE OF EXCESS SMOKE: Dirty oven! Daily cleaning is REQUIRED.

🧈 Oil & Butter Usage Guide

❌ NEVER SPRAY OIL DURING COOKING!
Interior is 475-500°F - oil spray can ignite!
Opening door stops cycle and loses heat.

✅ Correct Way to Use Oil/Butter

  1. BEFORE cooking: Spray or brush food at prep station
  2. Best oils: High-heat cooking spray (avocado, canola)
  3. For browning: Light coating only - excess causes smoke

Which Foods Need Oil?

  • Egg rolls: Essential for golden color
  • Potato skins: For crispiness
  • Salmon: Prevents sticking
  • Turkey legs: Golden crispy skin
  • Frozen fries: Already have oil - DON'T add more!
